In The Zone

A journal on mental health, track & field, and the inner life of competing.

This started because “This started when I noticed how easy it is to look okay as an athlete, even when you’re not.”.

Everyone talks about the grind, the work, the results. Nobody talks about the weird headspace before a race. The shame of a bad performance. The exhaustion of caring so much about something that doesn't care back.

In The Zone is where I write about that stuff. It's for track athletes specifically — but the psychology of competition is universal. If you've ever gotten in your own way, felt like a fraud, or talked yourself out of something you wanted: this is for you.

I write it because I needed to read it. Maybe you do too.
